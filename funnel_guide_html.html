Prologue: The Knowledge You Already Have

"You already know enough to make money online."

I know that sounds impossible, especially if you're reading this while sitting in a cubicle, dreading Monday mornings, or wondering how other people seem to have cracked some secret code that you weren't given access to. But here's what I need you to understand before we go any further:

The system has trained you to dismiss your own expertise.

Think about it. Right now, there are people paying good money to learn something you already know how to do. Maybe it's how to organize a chaotic schedule, how to meal prep for a family of five, how to train a rescue dog, or how to navigate office politics without losing your soul. Maybe it's something you learned in your day job that you think "everyone knows" but trust me—they don't.

I've been a serial entrepreneur my whole life, so I've learned this lesson more times than I care to admit. But one moment that really crystallized it for me was during my doctoral studies at Columbia University, bursting into my advisor Dr. Charles Basch's office every few days with yet another world-changing dissertation idea. He was incredibly kind and patient—honestly, I think he enjoyed my curiosity to explore every possible angle of making the world better.

But here's the thing: I'd still be a doctoral student right now if I hadn't finally just picked a damn thing and stuck to it long enough to get done.

And that's exactly what I need you to understand about making money online: just pick a damn thing and stick to it long enough to learn how it works.

But the moment that really changed my life happened in my car, listening to an audiobook version of Expert Secrets by Russell Brunson. Now, this might sound crazy, but I heard something in his voice—this frenzied energy, like he'd discovered a secret formula and was desperate for everyone to have it. No gatekeeping. No holding back. Just methodical, step-by-step breakdown of exactly how this whole "making money online" thing actually works.

And you know what hit me? We were twinning.

I'm a Black woman from New York, Haitian descent. He's a white man from the Midwest. We are worlds apart in every demographic imaginable. But that energy? That "this is too good to keep to myself" passion? I recognized it immediately because it's the same energy that's been driving me crazy for years.

"The perfect niche isn't out there waiting for you to discover it. The perfect niche is you."

Key #1: The Two Ways (And Only Two Ways) to Get Attention

When I was eight years old, I had a weird obsession with infomercials.

While other kids were watching cartoons, I was glued to the TV at 2 AM, mesmerized by people selling everything from kitchen gadgets to get-rich-quick schemes. But here's the thing—I didn't want what they were selling. I wanted to know how they were selling it.

Every single infomercial followed the exact same script: the problem, the testimonials ("if I can do it, you can too!"), the lifetime guarantee, the sense of urgency. It didn't matter if they were hawking a Thighmaster or a real estate program—the persuasion formula was identical.

Because back then, you had two options for reaching people: pay for it, or physically go find them.

I remember selling Cutco knives door-to-door (lasted exactly two weeks, by the way). On an aggressive day, you might knock on 50 doors and pray that one person would let you in. When I started my after-school enrichment company, I graduated to guerrilla marketing—standing outside schools with flyers, hoping parents would take one as they picked up their kids.

Good luck getting 200 people to take a flyer from you in a single day.

But now? A mediocre social media post gets 200 views in minutes. There's no way you could physically reach 200 people door-to-door in an entire day, let alone in the time it takes to hit "publish."

Everything changed. And yet, the fundamental truth about getting attention hasn't changed at all.
There are still only two ways to get people into your ecosystem:
Organic or Paid. Content or Ads. Earn the attention or buy the attention.

The Hook Point Reality

Here's what Brendan Kane calls your "hook point" in his book of the same name—those first three seconds when people either stop scrolling or keep walking. Whether you're creating organic content or running paid ads, you've got three seconds to grab someone's attention.

"Three seconds. That's shorter than it takes to read this sentence."

In digital marketing, it sounds more like this: "I know you're into home decor. I've got this fun quiz—answer a few questions about your preferences and style, and I'll give you a list of online shops that match your vibe."

They say: "Great, thanks!"

You say: "Perfect—just drop your email and I'll send it to your inbox."

This is the digital equivalent of that networking conversation. You've identified their interest, offered immediate value, and asked for a small commitment in return.

Then have it write your lead magnet offer using their actual words and phrases. When your copy sounds like their internal dialogue, conversion rates skyrocket.

Key #2: Why Funnels Matter to Making Money Online

But here's where the magic happens.

Out of 100 people who get that pitch, maybe 15 give you their email. And that 15% conversion only happens if you actually did your audience research and built something they genuinely want.

100 People See Your Offer
15 Give You Email
1 Becomes Customer

Out of those 15 people, maybe 5 like your free resource so much they book a sales call. Of those 5, maybe 1 person buys.

Now we're getting somewhere.

But here's the key—those other 14 people are still in your ecosystem. You can keep offering value, keep showing up, and over time, they'll start to know, like, and trust you too.

The Dollar-Per-Person Rule

For every engaged person you have in your funnel, you're looking at roughly $1 per month in potential revenue.

Think of your funnel as a house, and your lead magnet—that free resource you're offering—as the front door. It's what ushers people inside.

So why $1 per person?

Let's say you sell a $20 or $30 product. Maybe 1 person buys it, and 19 don't. Then over time, 2 more buy, and 15 still don't. That's how the numbers typically play out—so on average, each engaged person on your list is worth about a dollar a month.

Do the math: 10,000 people in your funnel equals $10,000 per month in potential revenue.

But here's the crucial part: this $1 per month rule only applies to engaged leads—people who opted in to your free offer and have shown real interest in what you do. You can't just buy 10,000 cold email addresses, add them to your system, and expect to make $10,000 a month. That's not how this works.

In the know-like-trust game, people who opt in for your lead magnet are officially in the "know" stage and maybe—just maybe—they're starting to like you. That's who the $1 per month rule applies to. Not cold lists. Not random emails. Real people with real interest who receive real value.

Key #3: Why You Need to Build It Yourself

And this is where the mindset shift kicks in.

You might be thinking: "Okay, fine—but I'll just hire someone on Fiverr or Upwork to build the funnel for me."

You can do that. But I promise you—you'll never make consistent money online that way.

Why?

Because this part—the funnel—is foundational. You don't have to become a full-time digital marketer, but you do need to know a few non-negotiable digital marketing skills.

It's like saying: "I'm a surgeon, but I don't know how to use a scalpel—someone else handles that part." Or "I'm a hairdresser, but I don't use scissors." Or "I'm a chef, but I don't touch the stove."

Those are the tools of the trade.

"Funnels are the tool of online business. They are the digital scalpel, the scissors, the stove."

And the moment you decide to sell digital products online, you become whatever you were before plus someone who creates digital content.

So go ahead and append that to your title: "I'm a teacher who creates digital content." "I'm a fitness coach who creates digital content." "I'm a hairstylist who creates digital content."

Because if you're going to invite people into your world, you need a house to welcome them into. And the front door of that house is your funnel.

You can't fully outsource the part where people enter your business. Not unless you know the system like the back of your hand. Otherwise, you're just standing outside your own house, watching opportunity walk right past you.

Key #4: Building Your Foundation - The Three-Page System

Here's what every audience-building funnel needs at minimum:

Page 1: The Opt-In Page

This is where people enter their email to receive your free resource. One clear headline, one compelling offer, one email field, one button. That's it.

Page 2: The Thank You Page

This is where people land after they opt in. It confirms their submission went through and delivers the free resource you promised.

Page 3: The Next Step Page

This is where you offer deeper engagement. Maybe it's a free strategy call, maybe it's a low-ticket product, maybe it's an invitation to join a group or community.

Here's an example:

Let's say your lead magnet was "5 Herbs for Natural Stress Relief." You've just solved one problem—they now know which herbs to use. But every time you solve one problem, you create a new one. Now they might be thinking: "This is great, but how do I know which herbs are right for my specific situation? Where do I even buy these? What dosage should I use?"

That new problem creates the perfect opportunity for your next step offer.

The Flow That Converts

When someone discovers your content—whether through organic posts or paid ads—they're essentially window shopping. Your job is to turn window shoppers into people who walk through your front door.

  • Your lead magnet is the invitation: "Come inside, I've got something you'll want to see."
  • Your thank you page is the welcome mat: "Thanks for coming in, here's what I promised you."
  • Your next step page is the deeper conversation: "Now that you've seen what I can do, want to go further?"

This isn't complicated. It's not fancy. But it works.

And once you understand this flow—really understand it—you can adapt it to sell anything. Courses, coaching, consulting, physical products, memberships. The structure stays the same; only the specifics change.

Your Digital Foundation

This funnel becomes the foundation for everything else you build online. Want to launch a course? Send people through your funnel first, then invite your engaged leads to buy. Want to fill a webinar? Your funnel audience becomes your webinar audience. Want to test a new product idea? Ask your funnel subscribers what they want.

You're not just building a lead capture system. You're building a direct line to people who have already raised their hand and said they're interested in what you have to offer.

And once you have that—once you have a predictable way to turn strangers into subscribers—everything else becomes possible.

That's the power of building your audience funnel. That's why this foundation matters more than any individual tactic, any specific platform, any particular strategy.

Because platforms change. Tactics become outdated. Strategies evolve.

"But the fundamental truth remains: people buy from people they know, like, and trust. Your funnel is how you systematically move strangers through that progression."

Build it right, and you'll never worry about where your next customer is coming from again.
